FAQ

What is Hack.UH - Get Your Hack On?

Get Your Hack On is happening May 13-15 at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. Students who are interested in web programming, graphic design, software and technology development are invited to participate.

Similar to "Startup Weekend Honolulu" and "City Camp," there will be outside judges from startups, and prizes. This is part of a series of workshops to help ready oneself to create a software application within ~50 hours. We will also be hosting a series of events to help the students prepare for the actual competition.

How do I participate?

If you are a student of the UH system campuses, you can register to participate here. The University of Hawaii and HI Capacity events are free and open to all students of the UH system.

What can I expect from the meetups happening before the hackathon?

Leading up to the main event is a twice-weekly series of workshops, talks, and hangouts, led by experts from the UH system, Hawaii's tech sector, and by the group's own members. The activities range from informal peer learning sessions to expert-driven seminars on a wide range of topics. Subjects for talks include Hawaii open data, local startup culture and the tech sector. Workshops focus on graphic design, web programming and system administration.
